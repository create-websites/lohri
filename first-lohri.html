The Significance and Celebration of First Lohri


Significance of First Lohri for Newly Weds, New Born and Others

Lohri is a popular Punjabi festival that is celebrated with great enthusiasm and fervor. It is a festival that marks the end of winter and the beginning of spring. The festival is celebrated on the 13th of January every year. The first Lohri is a special occasion for newlyweds, new-borns, and others as it marks the beginning of a new phase in their lives.

The first Lohri for newlyweds is a significant occasion as it is the first time that the bride visits her parents' home after marriage. It is a time for the bride to bond with her new family and to celebrate her new life. The first Lohri for a new-born is also a special occasion as it marks the baby's first winter. It is a time for the family to come together and celebrate the new addition to the family.

The first Lohri is also significant for other reasons. It is a time to celebrate new beginnings, new ventures, and new relationships. It is a time to let go of the past and to look forward to the future with hope and optimism.

How First Lohri is Celebrated

The celebration of Lohri begins with the lighting of a bonfire. People gather around the bonfire and offer prayers to the fire god, Agni. They throw sesame seeds, popcorn, and other offerings into the fire as a symbol of their gratitude and thanksgiving. They also sing traditional songs and dance around the fire.

People also exchange sweets and other delicacies with their friends and family. The most popular sweet that is exchanged during Lohri is gur rewri. It is a sweet made of jaggery and sesame seeds.

Lohri Festival Story

The story behind the Lohri festival is rooted in Punjabi folklore. According to the legend, there was a man named Dulla Bhatti who lived during the reign of Emperor Akbar. Dulla Bhatti was a Robin Hood-like figure who robbed the rich and helped the poor. He was also known for rescuing young girls who were being taken away to be sold as slaves.

One of the girls that Dulla Bhatti rescued was a young girl named Sundri. Sundri became a symbol of courage and bravery and was celebrated by the people of Punjab. The Lohri festival is celebrated in honor of Dulla Bhatti and Sundri. It is a time to celebrate courage, bravery, and the triumph of good over evil.
