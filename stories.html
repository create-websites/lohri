Lohri: A Festival of Light and Warmth

Lohri is a Punjabi harvest festival celebrated primarily in northern India, particularly in Punjab. It marks the end of the winter solstice and the beginning of longer days. The festival is characterized by bonfires, traditional songs, and the sharing of food.

Here are a few stories associated with Lohri:

The Legend of Dulla Bhatti

One of the most popular stories associated with Lohri is the legend of Dulla Bhatti, a 16th-century Punjabi folk hero. He is said to have protected the poor and oppressed from the tyrannical rule of the Mughals. It is believed that Dulla Bhatti would rescue kidnapped girls and return them to their families.

According to the legend, Dulla Bhatti once rescued a girl named Sundari from the clutches of the Mughal emperor Akbar. He hid her in a well and lit a bonfire to distract the Mughal soldiers. As a result, the people of the village celebrated Lohri to commemorate Dulla Bhatti's bravery and to express their gratitude for the girl's rescue.

The Story of the Sun God

Another story associated with Lohri is the story of the Sun God. It is said that the Sun God was once very angry with the people of the earth. As a result, the winter became extremely cold and harsh. The people prayed to the Sun God to return and bring warmth to the land.

Finally, the Sun God agreed to return, but only if the people promised to celebrate a festival in his honor. The people agreed, and Lohri was born. The festival was celebrated with bonfires to symbolize the warmth of the Sun God's return.

The Story of the Bride

In some regions of Punjab, Lohri is also celebrated as a pre-wedding ritual. It is believed that if a bride-to-be is blessed with a bonfire on Lohri night, she will be blessed with a happy and prosperous marriage.

The bonfire is also said to symbolize the burning away of any negativity or obstacles that may come between the couple.

These are just a few of the many stories associated with Lohri. The festival is a time for joy, celebration, and the strengthening of community bonds.
