"Lohri: A Vibrant Celebration of Punjab's Cultural Heritage"

"Lohri, a popular Punjabi festival, marks the end of winter and the arrival of longer days, traditionally associated with the harvest of rabi crops. The festival holds a strong connection to the tale of Dulla Bhatti, a hero of Punjabis, and is a culturally significant celebration involving dancing, singing, and feasting on traditional foods, praying for prosperity in the coming year."

Lohri: History, Mythology, and Cultural Significance
History Lohri is a popular Punjabi festival, celebrated by people from the Punjab region of South Asia. The origins of Lohri are many and link the festival to the Punjab region. Many people believe the festival commemorates the passing of the winter solstice. Lohri marks the end of winter and the arrival of longer days. It is traditionally associated with the harvest of the rabi crops.
Mythology The festival of Lohri holds a strong connection to the tale of Dulla Bhatti. Dulla Bhatti was a Muslim highway robber who lived in Punjab during the reign of Emperor Akbar. Besides robbing the rich, he rescued Hindu girls being forcibly taken to be sold in the slave market of the Middle East. He arranged their marriages to Hindu boys with Hindu rituals and provided them with dowries. Understandably, though a bandit, he became a hero of all Punjabis. So every other Lohri song has words to express gratitude to Dulla Bhatti.
Cultural Significance Lohri is a celebration of the arrival of longer days after the winter solstice. It is a cultural celebration on the mid-winter day, which is also the shortest day and the longest night. It is a day to pray for plenty and prosperity in the coming year. It is a culturally significant festival that is widely celebrated by dancing and singing around the bonfire. Lohri songs mention the Indian Sun god asking for heat and thanking him for his return. Another important aspect of Lohri is the food, with sesame seeds, gur (jaggery), moongphali (peanuts) and popcorn being important parts of the festival.
